• Name: Garry
  • Age: unknown (approx. 18-21)
  • Gender: male
  • Hair Color: lavender
  • Eye Color: blue

I don't want to lie ... but I don't want to tell the truth either... If you ... need help... I'll ... come running... Go on ahead, I'll be... fine... --Garry

Garry is the deuteragonist of the horror game "Ib" and the first character to join Ib's party. He is assumed to be around 18-21 years of age, though his age is never revealed in the game. Not much is known about Garry, because he doesn't tell Ib too much about himself.

Garry is tall and has a lean figure. He wears a long, dark blue, tattered coat that he claims to be "fashionable". He has light purple hair with darker purple lowlights, pale skin and blue eyes. His hair always covers his left eye. He wears pale brown pants and black shoes.

Garry talks in a refined manner and supposedly has a traditionally feminine personality (called lavender linguistics in Japan). Garry is mysterious, and often has mood swings (ex. when the mannequin head scares him, at first he is frightened and then angry) but when talking to Ib, he is kind and very nice to her from the first moment they meet. It also shows that he has a much gentler side when Ib faints and he covers her with his coat and even gives her a piece of lemon candy. As the game progresses, Garry continues to show signs of protectiveness over Ib.

Like Ib, Garry has a rose which represents his life, and as it wilts, so does Garry. Garry's rose is blue which is symbolic for impossible miracles (because blue roses don't exist naturally, so it seems impossible and miraculous).His rose has 10 petals, whereas Ib's has 5. The most likely reason is that he is an adult and Ib and Mary are just children. Also, another theory that Garry's rose is blue due to his personality. Blue represents Garry's kindness, exoticness, mysteriousness and mood swings, but could also represent loneliness.

When Ib first meets Garry, he is laying face down on the floor. He appears to be in a great amount of pain, moaning and not moving. After some exploring, Ib finds the Lady in Blue tearing apart a blue rose. After getting back the blue rose, healing it, and giving it to Garry, he joins Ib's party.


Garry seems pretty friendly, although a little shy. After he joins Ib's party, Garry aids Ib by clarifying words she does not know, protecting her, and often providing commentary. Garry is more easily startled in comparison to Ib by the events of the game. There is an instance where he falls over in shock and several other instances where he is given the opportunity to damage items in the gallery, which can lead to the bad endings. He particularly has a distaste for the blue "disturbing" dolls.


In the Gray Area, Ib collapses out of exhaustion in a hallway and Garry carries her into the "safe room" and covers her with his coat. When Ib wakes up, Garry asks if she is okay and, depending upon Ib's answer, gives her a piece of lemon candy from his coat pocket. Ib is Â able to talk to Garry directly in this room. He mentions that he is sorry he wasn't looking out for Ib as much as he should have been and gets surprised if Ib apologizes for causing him trouble.


In the Violet Area, Garry is followed by a disturbing blue doll after being separated from Mary and Ib. Garry becomes increasingly annoyed by this, and Ib has the option of either having him set it aside or kick it. Garry then proceeds to collect the 7 balls of paint needed for Ib to retreive the Brown Key at an upper level.
Garry discovers Mary's true identity in the Violet Area. Garry can also succeed or fail inside the Doll Room, which involves him searching for a key before time runs out. Winning the Doll Room event leads him up the stairs to later regroup with Ib and Mary. Failing the event causes him to hallucinate and talk to himself. He is later discovered by Ib, who snaps him back to reality by slapping him twice. If Garry's insanity is incurable, Ib will collapse in despair next to him and give up, leading to two possible bad endings ("Welcome to the World of Guertena" and "A Painting's Demise").


Ib gets the choice to talk with Garry again under the fake sunlight in the Sketchbook Area. Depending on whether the player is geared towards the best ending or the worst ending, Garry will either trade his rose for Ib's (and subsequentially die) or he will insist on going up the stairs into the hallway of Mary's painting.
It is possible to receive an ending where Mary kills Garry. If you approach Garry after his death, Ib will state that he is "sleeping". Depending on Ib's actions afterwards, the player may receive either "The Forgotten Portrait" ending, the "Ib All Alone" ending, or the "Together, Forever" ending.
